About: Izhikevich Spiking Neuron Model

> expand

This simulator implements the Izhikevich model (Izhikevich, 2003), a computationally efficient model that reproduces the spiking and bursting behavior of known types of cortical neurons. It combines the biological plausibility of Hodgkin-Huxley-type dynamics with the computational efficiency of integrate-and-fire neurons.

Equation Breakdown

First equation: v' = 0.04v2 + 5v + 140 - u + I

  • v = membrane potential (mV). This is what we measure in neurons.
  • 0.04v2 + 5v + 140 = quadratic term that captures the spike-generating currents. The coefficients were fit to match real cortical neurons.
  • u = recovery variable representing K+ currents and Na+ inactivation. It provides negative feedback.
  • I = input current (synaptic + tonic). This is how neurons receive external drive.

Second equation: u' = a(bv - u)

  • a = time scale of recovery. Smaller values = slower recovery. Fast-spiking neurons have larger a.
  • b = sensitivity of u to subthreshold fluctuations of v. Greater values couple v and u more strongly.
  • bv - u = the recovery variable u tracks the membrane potential with gain b.

Reset condition: if v >= 30mV, then v = c, u = u + d

  • 30mV threshold = when the membrane reaches this potential, a spike is fired.
  • c = after-spike reset value of v. More negative = deeper hyperpolarization after spike.
  • d = after-spike increment of u. Larger values = more adaptation (spike frequency adaptation).
Neuron Types Explained
  • RS (Regular Spiking) - Most common excitatory cortical neurons. Show spike-frequency adaptation.
  • IB (Intrinsically Bursting) - Layer 5 pyramidal cells. Fire initial burst, then single spikes.
  • CH (Chattering) - Fast rhythmic bursting cells. Fire stereotyped high-frequency bursts.
  • FS (Fast Spiking) - Inhibitory interneurons. Can sustain high firing rates without adaptation.
  • LTS (Low-Threshold Spiking) - Inhibitory cells that can fire from hyperpolarized states.
  • RZ (Resonator) - Exhibit subthreshold oscillations and resonance properties.
Reference: Izhikevich, E.M. (2003). "Simple Model of Spiking Neurons." IEEE Trans. Neural Networks, 14:1569-1572. [Paper + MATLAB code]
